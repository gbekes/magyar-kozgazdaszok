Contribute

Corrections, missing papers, missing authors, and popular-science write-ups — all welcome.

For authors of papers on this site

If you're one of the authors listed here, three specific asks:

  • Verify your entry. Open your author page and your paper pages. Are the summaries accurate? Is the policy-relevance framed right? Are key recent papers missing?
  • Send us popular-science versions of your work. If you have a VoxEU column, an index.hu / portfolio.hu / telex.hu / 444.hu piece, a KRTK blog post, or a Bruegel brief about one of your papers — send the URL. We'll add it to the paper page in a "Popular write-ups" section that's prominent for policymakers. This is arguably the highest-leverage thing you can do.
  • Suggest own-written highlights. If you have better 2–3 policy-relevance bullet points than our AI-drafted version, send them. Author-approved content gets a badge.

All of the above: either email (bekesg@ceu.edu) or file a PR on GitHub.

For policymakers, journalists, and other readers

  • Found a paper summary that's wrong, misleading, or unclear? File a GitHub issue or email.
  • Know of a Hungarian economist who should be on the site but isn't? Send their name (plus a link to their website if possible). Self-promotion is welcome.
  • Spotted a popular-science piece about one of our papers (in the Hungarian or international press) that isn't linked on the paper page? Send the URL.

Flag a missing paper or correction

Easiest path: email the editor at bekesg@ceu.edu with:

  • Paper title, authors, year, journal, DOI.
  • What's wrong or missing. Short is fine.

Submit via pull request

If you're comfortable with git, the data lives in JSON files in the repo:

  • One file per paper at data/papers/<slug>.json
  • One file per author at data/authors/<slug>.json

Slug format: firstauthor-secondauthor-year-journalshort (e.g., lindner-harasztosi-2019-aer). Use sample-paper.json as a template. Run python build.py locally to regenerate data/index.json, then open a PR.

Adding a popular-science link

Paper JSON files have a popular_links array. Each entry is a small object:

{
  "title": "Minimum wages don't destroy jobs in Hungary",
  "url": "https://voxeu.org/...",
  "outlet": "VoxEU"
}

Outlets we especially want to include: VoxEU, index.hu, portfolio.hu, telex.hu, 444.hu, KRTK blog, Bruegel. Academic blogs (Conversable Economist, Marginal Revolution) are also welcome.

Suggesting an author

Inclusion criterion: at least one publication in a top general or top field journal (see the journals list). "Hungarian" is broad: nationality, birthplace, or 3+ years primary affiliation.

How summaries get written

When a paper is added, the summary and policy-relevance fields start as ai-drafted. A human editor reviews, tightens, and flips them to human-reviewed. If the author signs off, status moves to author-approved and a badge appears on the paper page. Authors can submit their own draft at any time.

What we won't accept

  • Op-eds or blog posts as primary entries (but popular-science links to op-eds on existing papers are very welcome).
  • Papers that aren't yet publicly posted.
  • Summaries that copy-paste the abstract — we want plain language.
