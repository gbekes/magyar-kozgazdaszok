Contribute

Two paths in, depending on who you are.

If you're an author listed here

Email Gábor (bekesg@ceu.edu) with whatever you want changed, in free form, in English or Hungarian, whichever is easier.

Examples of things you can send:

  • "Please replace my bio with this text: …"
  • "On my minimum-wage paper, the policy bullets should be: (1)… (2)… (3)…"
  • "In the data description of paper X, it should be 1998–2012, not 2000–2012."
  • "Add this VoxEU link: URL"
  • "The transition-postcommunist tag doesn't fit my paper — remove it."
  • "Here is the complete Hungarian summary I'd prefer: …"

Send it in one language only, unless a factual change needs to be mirrored in the translation. We don't expect you to maintain both sides.

Gábor runs these through an integrated workflow: the edit lands in the site within a day or two, and the paper's "AI-drafted" warning is replaced with an "author-approved" status. No GitHub, no JSON, no template. Just write.

Technical detail for the curious: your email becomes a file in submissions/, Claude Code parses it and opens the right JSON edits, Gábor reviews the diff and pushes. The entire review gate is one editor reading the proposed change on a GitHub pull-request diff.

If you're anyone else

Readers, policymakers, journalists, students — all corrections and suggestions welcome, through one of these channels:

  • Open a GitHub issue. Best if you can describe the problem concretely (paper slug, what's wrong, what should be instead).
  • Email the editor at bekesg@ceu.edu with the same.
  • Open a pull request if you know git. The data lives in JSON files: one per paper at data/papers/<slug>.json, one per author at data/authors/<slug>.json. Run python build.py locally to refresh the index before opening the PR.

Things especially worth flagging

  • A missing Hungarian economist. If you think someone belongs on the site and isn't listed, send the name (ideally with a link to their website). Self-promotion welcome — the alternative is us missing you.
  • A missing paper by an already-listed author. Send the title / DOI / year.
  • A popular-science write-up of a listed paper (VoxEU especially; Bruegel, KRTK blog, or unambiguous Hungarian press pieces). These surface to policymakers more than academic papers do.
  • A wrong or misleading summary. The AI drafts are clearly labelled — flagging the worst of them is a real help.

How summaries move from AI to authored

Every paper starts with review_status: ai-drafted — an AI-written first draft. An editor reads and tightens it: human-reviewed. Once the author of the paper signs off (or sends their own version through the email workflow above), it becomes author-approved and the AI-drafted warning disappears from the page.

What we won't accept

  • Op-eds and blog posts as standalone entries. Popular-science links to such pieces, attached to an existing paper, are very welcome.
  • Papers that aren't yet publicly posted.
  • Summaries that copy-paste the abstract — we want plain-language rewrites.
