About

A searchable, policy-facing catalogue of research by top Hungarian economists. Built to close the gap between academic research and the people who could use it.

Why

A new Hungarian government takes office in 2026. Hungarian economists — at home and abroad — have published hundreds of peer-reviewed papers directly relevant to the choices that government will face: labor, demographics, skill formation, firm productivity, trade exposure, health, migration, housing, fiscal policy. Most of that work is invisible to the people who would use it. This site closes that gap.

Who is included

Economists admitted to the catalogue have at least one publication in a top general-interest or top field journal (full list in the SPEC). "Hungarian" is interpreted broadly: Hungarian nationality, born in Hungary, or three or more years of primary affiliation at a Hungarian institution. Diaspora is explicitly included.

Once an author is admitted, all their papers — published or working, recent or older — are eligible. You filter by topic, year, and method in the UI.

How the non-technical summaries are made

Each paper on the site carries three short, policy-facing fields: a plain-language summary (what the paper asked, what it found), a description of the data used, and a note on policy relevance. These are drafted with the help of AI from the abstract and (where available) the introduction and conclusion — and reviewed by a human editor before publication. Labels on each paper indicate review status: ai-drafted / human-reviewed / author-approved.

Summaries never copy-paste from the abstract. They answer three concrete questions: what did the paper ask, with what data, and what should a policymaker take from it?

Writing rules for summaries

  • No jargon. If a term must appear, define it in parentheses.
  • Numbers and effect sizes with units, not vague language.
  • Name the country studied explicitly.
  • Policy relevance is specific: who would use this, how.
  • External-validity caveats are included rather than glossed.

Languages

The site is bilingual: English at /, Hungarian at /hu/. The data is shared — only the UI strings and the two summary fields differ. Hungarian summaries are being rolled out gradually; where a translation is missing, the English summary is shown with a note.

What's missing today

This is a scaffold, not a finished product. Today's catalogue holds a small seed set of papers and authors. Paper coverage grows each week. If you spot a missing paper, a missing author, or an error — please write in. See Contribute.
